chapter 5
Income from Salary (Grid B)
Question 1 Under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001, what is the basis of chargeability for income under the head "Salary"? A) Accrual basis B) Receipt basis C) Whichever is earlier between accrual or receipt D) Matching concept basis Correct Answer: B Explanation: Salary is taxable strictly on a receipt basis; any salary received by an employee in a tax year shall be chargeable to tax in that year.
Question 2 An employee incurred expenses on travelling for the employer's business and was paid a specific allowance solely to cover those official duties. How will this allowance be treated for tax purposes? A) It will be fully included in the taxable salary. B) 50% of the allowance will be taxable. C) It will be completely excluded from the taxable salary. D) It will be taxed under "Income from Other Sources". Correct Answer: C Explanation: Any allowance provided to an employee is included in salary, excluding any allowance solely expended in the performance of duties of employment.
Question 3 Mr. Zaid is provided with a rent-free accommodation by his employer. His basic salary is Rs. 120,000 per month, but his minimum of time scale (MTS) is Rs. 100,000 per month. The annual fair market rent of the accommodation is Rs. 500,000. What amount will be added to his taxable salary for the accommodation? A) Rs. 500,000 B) Rs. 540,000 C) Rs. 648,000 D) Rs. 450,000 Correct Answer: B Explanation: The amount to be included is the higher of the fair market rent (Rs. 500,000) or 45% of the MTS (45% x Rs. 100,000 x 12 = Rs. 540,000). Therefore, Rs. 540,000 will be added.
Question 4 A company provided a leased 1300cc car to its marketing manager for both official and private use. The fair market value of the car at the commencement of the lease was Rs. 3,000,000. What amount will be included in the manager's taxable salary for this perquisite? A) Rs. 300,000 B) Rs. 150,000 C) Rs. 0 D) Rs. 450,000 Correct Answer: B Explanation: For a leased vehicle used partly for personal and partly for official use, 5% of the fair market value (FMV) of the motor vehicle at the commencement of the lease is added to the salary (5% of 3,000,000 = Rs. 150,000).
Question 5 ABC Ltd. granted a loan of Rs. 2,000,000 to an employee on 1 July 2025 at a concessional interest rate of 4% per annum. The benchmark rate specified in the law is 10%. What amount will be added to the employee's taxable salary for the tax year 2026? A) Rs. 200,000 B) Rs. 120,000 C) Rs. 80,000 D) Nil, because loans up to Rs. 2,000,000 are exempt. Correct Answer: B Explanation: Since the loan exceeds the Rs. 1,000,000 exemption limit, the difference between the benchmark rate (10%) and the actual rate paid (4%) is taxable. 6% of Rs. 2,000,000 = Rs. 120,000.
Question 6 Mr. Tariq's employer agreed to bear the tax chargeable on his salary. According to Section 12(3) of the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001, how should this be treated? A) The tax borne by the employer is exempt from tax for the employee. B) The tax borne by the employer is treated as an additional benefit and the salary must be grossed up. C) The tax borne by the employer is treated as a deductible allowance. D) It is taxable as a separate block of income at a flat 15% rate. Correct Answer: B Explanation: Where an employer agrees to pay the tax chargeable on an employee's salary, the amount paid is treated as an additional benefit, and the salary income must be grossed up by the amount of tax payable by the employer.
Question 7 Mr. Fahad resigned from his job and received Rs. 500,000 from an unapproved gratuity fund. He has never received any gratuity before. What amount of this gratuity will be exempt from tax? A) Rs. 500,000 (Fully exempt) B) Rs. 300,000 C) Rs. 250,000 D) Rs. 75,000 Correct Answer: D Explanation: Gratuity received from an unapproved scheme or fund is exempt up to Rs. 75,000 or 50% of the amount receivable (Rs. 250,000), whichever is lower. Thus, Rs. 75,000 is exempt.
Question 8 An employee received a substantial Golden Handshake payment upon termination of employment. Under Section 12(6), the employee has the option to have this amount taxed at: A) A flat rate of 10%. B) The average rate of tax paid over the three preceding tax years. C) The rate applicable to the highest tax slab. D) Zero percent, as it is a capital receipt. Correct Answer: B Explanation: An employee receiving compensation on termination (including a golden handshake) may elect to have the amount taxed at the average rate of tax calculated over the three preceding tax years.
Question 9 Mr. Ali receives a basic salary of Rs. 2,000,000 per annum and a medical allowance of Rs. 250,000 per annum. His employer does NOT provide any free medical facility. What is his taxable medical allowance? A) Rs. 250,000 B) Rs. 200,000 C) Rs. 50,000 D) Nil Correct Answer: C Explanation: Medical allowance is exempt up to 10% of the basic salary (10% of 2,000,000 = Rs. 200,000). The remaining amount (Rs. 250,000 - Rs. 200,000) of Rs. 50,000 is taxable.
Question 10 In the case of a Recognized Provident Fund, what is the exemption limit for the employer's contribution to the fund? A) It is fully exempt with no limits. B) It is fully taxable. C) Lower of Rs. 150,000 or 1/10th of (Basic Salary + Dearness Allowance). D) Higher of 16% of accumulated balance or 1/3rd of basic salary. Correct Answer: C Explanation: For a Recognized Provident Fund, the employer's yearly contribution is exempt up to Rs. 150,000 or 1/10th of (Basic Salary + Dearness Allowance), whichever is lower.
Question 11 An employee was granted an option to acquire 5,000 shares of his employer's company under an Employee Share Scheme. He paid Rs. 10,000 for the option. He later exercised the option and paid Rs. 50 per share to acquire them when the fair market value was Rs. 80 per share. What amount will be included in his salary income? A) Rs. 150,000 B) Rs. 140,000 C) Rs. 250,000 D) Rs. 400,000 Correct Answer: B Explanation: The taxable amount is the FMV of the shares minus the consideration paid for the shares minus the consideration paid for the option. (5,000 x 80) - (5,000 x 50) - 10,000 = 400,000 - 250,000 - 10,000 = Rs. 140,000.
Question 12 Under an Employee Share Scheme, if shares issued to an employee are subject to a restriction on transfer, when is the amount chargeable to tax? A) In the tax year the option is granted. B) In the tax year the shares are issued, regardless of the restriction. C) In the tax year the employee gains the free right to transfer the shares or disposes of them, whichever is earlier. D) In the tax year the employee retires from the company. Correct Answer: C Explanation: If shares are subject to a restriction on transfer, no amount is chargeable to tax until the earlier of the time the employee has the free right to transfer the shares or the employee disposes of the shares.
Question 13 According to recent amendments in the tax law regarding pension income, which of the following individuals will have their pension income treated as fully exempt? A) Any citizen of Pakistan receiving a pension under Rs. 15 million. B) A recipient who is over 70 years old. C) An individual who continues to work for the same employer after retirement. D) Pension is now fully taxable for all individuals regardless of age. Correct Answer: B Explanation: While the general exemption on pension income has been withdrawn, a pension received by an individual who is over 70 years old remains fully exempt.
Question 14 Mr. Junaid's total deductions allowed under the head "Salary" for earning his employment income amounted to Rs. 50,000 (for professional books and tools). How much of this will be allowed as a deduction against his salary income? A) Rs. 50,000 B) Rs. 25,000 C) Nil D) 10% of his basic salary Correct Answer: C Explanation: In computing income under the head salary, no deduction is allowed for any expenses incurred by the employee in earning such income.
Question 15 A taxpayer earning income solely from salary has a taxable salary of Rs. 1,100,000. Under the First Schedule, what is the tax rate applied to the amount exceeding Rs. 600,000? A) 0% B) 1% C) 11% D) 15% Correct Answer: B Explanation: For a salaried individual where taxable income exceeds Rs. 600,000 but does not exceed Rs. 1,200,000, the rate of tax is 1% of the amount exceeding Rs. 600,000.

chapter 6
Income from Property (Grid B)
Question 1 Under Section 15 of the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001, which of the following amounts is explicitly included in the definition of "rent" for the purpose of taxation under the head 'Income from Property'? A) Consideration received for the sale of a building. B) Any forfeited deposit paid under a contract for the sale of land or a building. C) Amount received for the provision of utilities and amenities connected with the building. D) Consideration received for leasing out plant and machinery. Correct Answer: B Explanation: The definition of rent specifically includes any forfeited deposit paid under a contract for the sale of land or a building.
Question 2 Mr. Ali leases out his factory building along with the installed plant and machinery for a composite monthly rent of Rs. 300,000. Under which head of income will this composite rent be chargeable to tax? A) Income from Property B) Income from Business C) Capital Gains D) Income from Other Sources Correct Answer: D Explanation: Where any amount is included in rent received for the lease of a building together with plant and machinery, such amount shall be chargeable to tax under 'Income from Other Sources', not Income from Property.
Question 3 Mr. Kashif let out his commercial property at a monthly rent of Rs. 100,000. However, the fair market rent for a similar property in the same area is Rs. 130,000 per month. What amount will be considered as gross rent chargeable to tax for the full tax year? A) Rs. 1,200,000 B) Rs. 1,560,000 C) Rs. 360,000 D) Rs. 2,760,000 Correct Answer: B Explanation: Where the rent received or receivable is less than the fair market rent for the property, the person shall be treated as having received the fair market rent (Rs. 130,000 x 12 = Rs. 1,560,000).
Question 4 Two brothers jointly own a commercial plaza. Their respective shares in the property are NOT definite and ascertainable. For income tax purposes, how will the rental income from this property be assessed? A) It will be divided equally between the two brothers and taxed individually. B) The property will be considered as being jointly owned by an Association of Persons (AOP) and taxed as per the principles of an AOP. C) The elder brother will pay the entire tax on the property. D) The rental income will be exempt from tax due to a lack of definite ownership shares. Correct Answer: B Explanation: Where a property is owned by two or more persons and their respective shares are not definite and ascertainable, the property will be considered as being jointly owned by an AOP, and the taxable income will be computed under AOP taxation principles.
Question 5 Mr. A received Rs. 2,400,000 on 1 July 2025 as two years' advance rent for his building. How much of this amount will be chargeable to tax under 'Income from Property' in the tax year 2026? A) Rs. 2,400,000 B) Rs. 240,000 C) Rs. 1,200,000 D) Nil, because advance rent is taxed in the year the tenancy ends. Correct Answer: C Explanation: Rent relating to a tax year, whether received or receivable, is chargeable to tax in that specific tax year. Therefore, advance rent must be apportioned, and only the rent relating to the current tax year (Rs. 1,200,000) will be taxed in tax year 2026.
Question 6 What is the statutory rate for the repair allowance allowed as a deduction against rent chargeable to tax under the head "Income from Property" for an individual? A) 10% of the rent chargeable to tax. B) Actual repair expenses incurred during the year. C) 1/5th (20%) of the rent chargeable to tax. D) 1/10th of the rent chargeable to tax. Correct Answer: C Explanation: A statutory allowance equal to one-fifth (20%) of the rent chargeable to tax is allowed for repairs to a building, irrespective of the actual repair expenses incurred.
Question 7 Mr. Saad pays Rs. 150,000 annually to an agency for rent collection and administration of his plaza. His gross rent chargeable to tax for the year is Rs. 3,000,000. What is the maximum deduction he is allowed to claim for administration and collection charges? A) Rs. 150,000 B) Rs. 120,000 C) Rs. 600,000 D) Nil Correct Answer: B Explanation: The deduction for administration and collection charges cannot exceed 4% of the rent chargeable to tax. Since the actual expense is Rs. 150,000, but 4% of Rs. 3,000,000 is Rs. 120,000, the deduction is restricted to the lower amount of Rs. 120,000.
Question 8 Mr. Dawood incurred Rs. 180,000 as actual repair and maintenance expenses on his rented property during the year. The gross rent chargeable to tax for the property is Rs. 2,000,000. What amount of repair allowance will he be allowed to deduct while computing his taxable income? A) Rs. 180,000 B) Rs. 200,000 C) Rs. 400,000 D) Nil Correct Answer: C Explanation: For individuals and AOPs, the law allows a flat statutory repair allowance equal to 1/5th (20%) of the gross rent chargeable to tax, irrespective of the actual expenses. Therefore, 1/5th of Rs. 2,000,000 = Rs. 400,000 is allowed.
Question 9 Mr. Z received a non-adjustable advance of Rs. 1,000,000 from a tenant in tax year 2023. The tenant vacated the premises in January 2026, and the advance was refunded. A new tenant moved in on 1 February 2026, paying a new non-adjustable advance of Rs. 1,500,000. What amount of the new advance will be added to the rent chargeable to tax in tax year 2026? A) Rs. 150,000 B) Rs. 120,000 C) Rs. 100,000 D) Rs. 1,200,000 Correct Answer: B Explanation: The previously taxed amount of the old advance (3 years x Rs. 100,000 = Rs. 300,000) must be subtracted from the new advance. The balance (Rs. 1,500,000 - Rs. 300,000 = Rs. 1,200,000) is then divided by 10. Therefore, Rs. 120,000 is chargeable to tax in tax year 2026.
Question 10 Which of the following local taxes paid in respect of a rented property is NOT allowed as a deduction against 'Income from Property'? A) Property tax paid to the local municipality. B) Water tax and conservancy tax. C) Income tax payable under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001. D) Ground rent paid in respect of the property. Correct Answer: C Explanation: Any local rate, tax, charge, or cess paid in respect of the property is deductible, excluding any tax payable under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001.
Question 11 If a taxpayer claims an admissible deduction for an expense (e.g., insurance premium) but fails to actually pay that liability within a specific period, the unpaid amount is added back to his 'Income from Property'. What is the statutory time limit before this add-back occurs? A) One year from the end of the tax year in which the deduction was allowed. B) Three years from the end of the tax year in which the deduction was allowed. C) Five years from the date the expense was incurred. D) The amount is never added back. Correct Answer: B Explanation: If an allowed liability is not paid within three years of the end of the tax year in which the deduction was allowed, the unpaid amount becomes chargeable to tax under 'Income from Property' in the first tax year following the end of those three years.
Question 12 Which of the following is a mandatory condition that must be met to successfully claim a deduction for unpaid (bad) rent? A) The tenant is still occupying the property but has signed a promissory note. B) The unpaid rent was never included in the person's taxable income in previous years. C) The tenancy was bona fide, the tenant has vacated, and legal proceedings for recovery have been instituted or are deemed useless. D) The landlord must write off the rent in his bank statements. Correct Answer: C Explanation: To claim an allowance for unpaid rent, the tenancy must be bona fide, the defaulting tenant must have vacated (or steps taken to compel vacation), reasonable steps for legal recovery must have been initiated, and the unpaid rent must have been previously included in taxable income.
Question 13 Miss Sara's commercial property generated a gross rent of Rs. 1,000,000 for the tax year. She incurred the following actual expenses: Property Tax Rs. 50,000, Insurance Premium Rs. 30,000, Actual Repairs Rs. 100,000, and Legal fees to defend the property's title Rs. 20,000. Calculate her net taxable Income from Property. A) Rs. 800,000 B) Rs. 700,000 C) Rs. 880,000 D) Rs. 600,000 Correct Answer: B Explanation: Gross Rent = Rs. 1,000,000. Less statutory deductions: Repair allowance (1/5th of 1,000,000 = Rs. 200,000 - actual repair of 100,000 is ignored), Property Tax (Rs. 50,000), Insurance (Rs. 30,000), Legal fees (Rs. 20,000). Total deductions = Rs. 300,000. Net Taxable Income = Rs. 700,000.
Question 14 Mr. Usman owns 50 acres of agricultural land in Mirpur. He does not cultivate it himself but leases it to a tenant farmer for an annual rent of Rs. 2,500,000. How is this rental income treated under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001? A) It is fully taxable under the head 'Income from Property'. B) It is taxable as 'Income from Other Sources'. C) It is treated as agricultural income and is entirely exempt from income tax. D) It is taxed at a flat rate of 15%. Correct Answer: C Explanation: Any rent received by the owner of land used for agricultural purposes is treated as agricultural income and is exempt from tax. It is not necessary for the owner to cultivate the land himself to claim this exemption.
Question 15 XYZ Ltd. owns a residential house and provides it as a rent-free accommodation to its Marketing Director. The fair market rent of the house is Rs. 1,200,000. Under the self-hiring rules, what is the tax implication for XYZ Ltd. (the employer) regarding this property? A) XYZ Ltd. must declare Rs. 1,200,000 as Income from Property. B) XYZ Ltd. will not be considered to have earned any rental income from this property, and there are no tax consequences for the employer. C) XYZ Ltd. must pay a 10% penalty tax. D) XYZ Ltd. can claim the fair market rent as an administrative expense deduction. Correct Answer: B Explanation: When an employer provides its own residential house to an employee rent-free, the fair market rent is added to the employee's taxable salary as a perquisite. However, the employer is not considered to have earned any rental income from the property, meaning there are no tax consequences for the employer
