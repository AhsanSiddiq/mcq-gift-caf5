chapter 9 Capital Gains (Grid B)
Question 1 Under Section 37(5), which of the following assets is specifically INCLUDED in the definition of a "capital asset"? A) Stock-in-trade held for the purpose of business. B) A personal motor vehicle used by the taxpayer's family. C) A painting or work of art kept for personal use. D) A depreciable asset used in the taxpayer's business. Correct Answer: C Explanation: Capital asset excludes movable property held for personal use, EXCEPT for specific items like a painting, sculpture, drawing, jewelry, rare manuscript, postage stamp, coin, medallion, or antique. Thus, a painting remains a capital asset even if held for personal use.
Question 2 Which of the following scenarios does NOT constitute a "disposal" of an asset under Section 75 of the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001? A) The transmission of an asset by succession or under a will. B) The application of a business asset to personal use. C) The cancellation, redemption, or destruction of an asset. D) Pledging an asset as collateral to secure a bank loan. Correct Answer: D Explanation: Pledging an asset as collateral does not mean the owner has parted with the ownership. Disposal involves selling, exchanging, transferring, transmitting by succession, or applying a business asset to personal use.
Question 3 Mr. Zaid sold an antique coin collection at a loss of Rs. 80,000 and his wife's jewelry at a gain of Rs. 150,000 during the tax year. What is the net capital gain chargeable to tax? A) Rs. 70,000 B) Rs. 150,000 C) Rs. 230,000 D) Nil Correct Answer: B Explanation: According to Section 38(5), no loss is recognized on the disposal of specific capital assets such as a painting, jewelry, coin, medallion, or antique. Therefore, the loss of Rs. 80,000 on the coin collection cannot be deducted, and the full gain of Rs. 150,000 on the jewelry is taxable.
Question 4 If a taxpayer sustains a general capital loss (under Section 59) during a tax year that cannot be fully set off, what is the maximum period it can be carried forward? A) It cannot be carried forward. B) Up to 3 immediately succeeding tax years. C) Up to 6 immediately succeeding tax years. D) Indefinitely. Correct Answer: C Explanation: The amount of capital loss which cannot be set-off shall be carried forward up to six (6) tax years immediately succeeding the tax year for which the loss was first computed. (Note: For losses on securities under Sec 37A, the carry-forward period is 3 years).
Question 5 For an active taxpayer, what is the capital gains tax rate on the disposal of an immovable property acquired on or after 1 July 2024? A) 0% if held for more than 6 years. B) 10% regardless of the holding period. C) 15% regardless of the holding period. D) Taxed at normal progressive slab rates. Correct Answer: C Explanation: For properties acquired on or after 1 July 2024, a flat rate of 15% applies for persons appearing on the Active Taxpayers' List, completely regardless of the holding period.
Question 6 A resident active taxpayer purchased 10,000 shares of a listed company. He later disposed of these shares through a private arrangement (off-market), and the transaction was NOT settled through the National Clearing Company of Pakistan Limited (NCCPL). How will the capital gain on these shares be taxed? A) It will be taxed as a separate block of income at 15%. B) It will be taxed under the Normal Tax Regime (NTR) at applicable progressive slab rates. C) It will be completely exempt from tax. D) It will be taxed at a flat rate of 12.5%. Correct Answer: B Explanation: A proviso in Section 37A states that the disposal of listed company shares made otherwise than through a registered stock exchange and NOT settled through NCCPL falls outside the ambit of Section 37A (Separate Block) and is instead taxed under Section 37 under the Normal Tax Regime (NTR).
Question 7 When a capital gain arises on a "Market-Based Transaction" of a security (settled through NCCPL), how are incidental expenses like brokerage and commission treated for calculating the gain? A) Actual brokerage and commission expenses paid are deducted from the sale proceeds. B) No deductions for expenses are allowed under any circumstances. C) A notional expense equal to 0.5% of the sale proceeds and 0.5% of the security's cost is applied instead of actual charges. D) A flat fixed deduction of Rs. 10,000 is allowed. Correct Answer: C Explanation: In the case of a Market-Based Transaction involving a security, a notional expense equal to 0.5% of the sale proceeds and 0.5% of the security's cost will be applied instead of actual charges like brokerage, commission, and other incidental expenses.
Question 8 Mr. Tariq sold a specific listed security at a significant loss. Two weeks later, he repurchased the exact same security to maintain his portfolio risk profile while artificially realizing the capital loss to reduce his tax liability. What is this transaction called, and is the capital loss admissible? A) Tax swap sale; the loss is admissible. B) Cross trade; the loss is inadmissible. C) Wash sale; the loss is inadmissible. D) Short selling; the loss is admissible. Correct Answer: C Explanation: This is defined as a "Wash Sale" (where a capital loss is realized and the same security is purchased within a one-month period by the same investor to maintain the portfolio). Under Rule 13F, capital loss adjustments for Wash Sales are not admissible.
Question 9 Mr. A acquired shares in a private (unlisted) company. Under Section 37(6), what is his obligation regarding withholding tax at the time of paying the seller or registering the shares? A) He must deduct advance adjustable tax at the rate of 10% of the fair market value of the shares. B) He must deduct tax at 15% of the actual capital gain earned by the seller. C) No withholding tax applies to the sale of private company shares. D) He must deduct tax at 5% of the face value of the shares. Correct Answer: A Explanation: The person acquiring a capital asset, being shares of a company (other than listed company shares settled through NCCPL), shall deduct advance adjustable tax at the rate of 10% of the fair market value of the shares at the time of payment or registration, whichever is earlier.
Question 10 Under Section 7E, resident persons are subjected to a tax on the "deemed income" arising from their capital assets (immovable property) situated in Pakistan. How is this deemed income and the corresponding tax computed? A) Deemed income is 10% of FMV; taxed at 15%. B) Deemed income is 5% of FMV; taxed at 20% (Effective tax of 1% of FMV). C) Deemed income is 20% of FMV; taxed at 5%. D) Deemed income is equal to the annual fair market rent; taxed at standard slab rates. Correct Answer: B Explanation: Deemed income shall be computed as 5% of the Fair Market Value (FBR/DC rate) of capital assets situated in Pakistan. The rate of tax on such deemed income is 20%, which translates into an effective tax of exactly 1% of the property's Fair Market Value.
Question 11 Which of the following immovable properties is NOT excluded from the levy of tax on deemed income under Section 7E? A) One immovable property owned by the resident person. B) Self-owned business premises from where the business is carried out by an active taxpayer. C) A vacant residential plot worth Rs. 40 million, which is the person's second property and not rented out. D) Self-owned agricultural land where agricultural activity is carried out. Correct Answer: C Explanation: The vacant residential plot (which is a second property, not rented out, and exceeds the Rs. 25 million aggregate threshold) does not meet any of the exclusion criteria under Section 7E and will be subject to the deemed income tax.
Question 12 Mr. Junaid sold his personal use car for Rs. 2,000,000, which he had originally purchased for Rs. 1,500,000. He also sold an antique watch for Rs. 150,000, which his mother had gifted him (her original cost was Rs. 50,000). What is his total taxable capital gain? A) Rs. 500,000 B) Rs. 600,000 C) Rs. 100,000 D) Nil Correct Answer: C Explanation: A personal vehicle is specifically excluded from the definition of a capital asset, so the Rs. 500,000 gain on the car is exempt. The antique watch is a capital asset. For assets acquired by gift from a relative, the original cost of the transferor (Rs. 50,000) is taken as the cost. Capital gain = 150,000 - 50,000 = Rs. 100,000.
Question 13 Mr. X purchased an open commercial plot having a fair market value of Rs. 12,000,000. He paid Rs. 6,000,000 through a crossed banking instrument and the remaining Rs. 6,000,000 in physical cash. Under Section 75A, what are the tax implications for computing capital gains upon future disposal? A) The entire Rs. 12,000,000 will be allowed as the cost of acquisition. B) The Rs. 6,000,000 paid in cash shall not be treated as cost, and he must pay a penalty of 5% of the FMV/DC rate. C) The transaction is completely void, and no capital gain or loss will ever be recognized. D) The cash portion will be taxed as 'Income from Other Sources'. Correct Answer: B Explanation: Under Section 75A, purchasing immovable property having a value greater than Rs. 5 million in cash means the cash amount shall not be treated as cost for computing gain on sale, and the person shall pay a penalty of 5% of the FBR value or DC rate (whichever is higher).
Question 14 Mr. Ali inherited 5,000 shares of a listed company from his father on 1 July 2025. His father was the original allottee of these shares at Rs. 10 per share. The fair market value of these shares at the time of inheritance was Rs. 15 per share. Mr. Ali sold these shares on 30 January 2026 for Rs. 20 per share via NCCPL. What cost will be used to compute Mr. Ali's capital gain? A) Rs. 10 per share (the original cost to his father). B) Rs. 15 per share (the FMV at the time of inheritance). C) Rs. 20 per share. D) Nil, because inherited assets have zero cost. Correct Answer: A Explanation: For assets acquired by inheritance or gift from relatives, there is no acquisition cost for the person acquiring the asset. Therefore, the original cost of the transferor (the father's cost of Rs. 10 per share) is treated as the cost of the asset for computing the capital gain.
Question 15 A taxpayer sustains a loss on the disposal of listed securities (settled via NCCPL). Under the rules for Section 37A, what is the maximum carry-forward period for this specific loss? A) It cannot be carried forward at all. B) Up to 3 immediately succeeding tax years. C) Up to 6 immediately succeeding tax years. D) Indefinitely. Correct Answer: B Explanation: Loss sustained by a person on the disposal of securities (under Sec 37A) can only be carried forward up to three (3) tax years immediately succeeding the tax year for which the loss was first computed. (General capital losses under Section 59 have a 6-year limit).

chapter 10 Income from Other Sources (Grid B)
Question 1 Under the Income Tax Ordinance, 2001, what is the basis of chargeability for income falling under the head "Income from Other Sources"? A) Accrual basis B) Receipt basis C) Whichever is earlier between accrual and receipt D) The method of accounting regularly employed by the taxpayer Correct Answer: B Explanation: Income from other sources is strictly chargeable to tax on a 'receipt basis'. Any income from other sources which is accrued for a tax year but is not received is not chargeable in that tax year.
Question 2 Mr. Kashif leased out a commercial building along with the plant and machinery installed in it for a composite rent of Rs. 500,000 per month. Under which head of income will this composite rent be chargeable to tax? A) Income from Property B) Income from Business C) Capital Gains D) Income from Other Sources Correct Answer: D Explanation: Income from the lease of any building together with plant or machinery is specifically chargeable to tax under the head "Income from Other Sources".
Question 3 Mr. Zaid received a lump-sum amount of Rs. 3,000,000 as consideration for vacating the possession of a rented commercial building. He did not pay any amount to originally acquire possession of the building. How will this amount be taxed? A) The entire Rs. 3,000,000 will be taxed in the year of receipt under 'Income from Property'. B) It will be completely exempt from tax as a capital receipt. C) Rs. 300,000 will be chargeable to tax under 'Income from Other Sources' in the year of receipt and the following nine tax years. D) Rs. 3,000,000 will be taxed under 'Capital Gains'. Correct Answer: C Explanation: Any amount received as consideration for vacating the possession of a building (reduced by any amount paid to acquire possession) shall be chargeable to tax under "Income from Other Sources" in the tax year in which it was received and the following nine tax years in equal proportion (i.e., 1/10th or Rs. 300,000 each year).
Question 4 Mr. Ali received a cash gift of Rs. 1,000,000 from his father, and a gift of an antique painting worth Rs. 500,000 from his mother. What is the tax treatment of these gifts? A) Both gifts are fully exempt from tax. B) The cash gift is taxable under 'Income from Other Sources', while the painting is exempt. C) Both gifts are taxable under 'Income from Other Sources'. D) The painting is taxable, while the cash gift is exempt. Correct Answer: B Explanation: Gifts received in the form of assets from relatives (like an ancestor) are exempt from tax. However, any gift received in cash even from relatives is taxable under the head "Income from Other Sources".
Question 5 A taxpayer received a valuable gift from his current tenant. Under which head of income will this gift be classified and taxed? A) Income from Salary B) Income from Other Sources C) Income from Property D) Income from Business Correct Answer: C Explanation: A gift received from a tenant is considered a form of rental income linked to the property, and is therefore taxable under the head "Income from Property".
Question 6 XYZ (Pvt.) Limited extended a loan of Rs. 3,000,000 to one of its shareholders on 1 July 2025. The company’s accumulated profits on that date were Rs. 2,200,000. How much of this loan will be treated as a "dividend" for the shareholder under Section 2(19)? A) Rs. 3,000,000 B) Rs. 800,000 C) Rs. 2,200,000 D) Nil Correct Answer: C Explanation: Any payment by a private company by way of advance or loan to a shareholder is treated as a dividend to the extent to which the company possesses accumulated profits. Therefore, Rs. 2,200,000 is treated as a dividend.
Question 7 Which of the following distributions or remittances is explicitly EXCLUDED from the definition of a "dividend"? A) Any distribution by a company of accumulated profits to its shareholders. B) Remittance of after-tax profit by a branch of a foreign company operating in Pakistan. C) Remittance of after-tax profit by a branch of a Petroleum Exploration and Production (E&P) foreign company operating in Pakistan. D) Any distribution made to the shareholders of a company on its liquidation. Correct Answer: C Explanation: Remittance of after-tax profit by a branch of a Petroleum Exploration and Production (E&P) foreign company operating in Pakistan is explicitly excluded from the definition of dividend.
Question 8 Mr. A, an individual, received a dividend of Rs. 170,000 (net of 15% tax) from a listed company. No Zakat was deducted. What is his gross taxable dividend income, and what is the final tax liability on this amount? A) Gross dividend Rs. 170,000; Tax Rs. 25,500 B) Gross dividend Rs. 200,000; Tax Rs. 30,000 C) Gross dividend Rs. 200,000; Tax Rs. 0 D) Gross dividend Rs. 170,000; Tax Rs. 0 Correct Answer: B Explanation: The gross dividend is calculated by grossing up the net receipt: 170,000 / (1 - 0.15) = 170,000 / 0.85 = Rs. 200,000. The tax rate for individuals on normal dividends is 15%, so the tax liability is Rs. 30,000 (which has already been deducted at source).
Question 9 Mr. Bashir, a resident individual, earned a profit on debt of Rs. 4,500,000 from his savings account in a commercial bank. He has no other source of income. How will this profit on debt be taxed? A) Taxable under the Normal Tax Regime (NTR) at applicable slab rates. B) Taxable as a separate block of income at a flat rate of 15%. C) Fully exempt from tax. D) Taxable under 'Income from Business'. Correct Answer: B Explanation: For individuals and AOPs, profit on debt up to Rs. 5 million received from prescribed persons (like banks) is taxable as a separate block at the rate of 15% under section 7B.
Question 10 Mr. Junaid, a resident individual, earned a profit on debt of Rs. 7,000,000 during the tax year. How will this income be classified and taxed? A) The entire Rs. 7,000,000 is taxable as a separate block at 15%. B) Rs. 5,000,000 is taxed as a separate block at 15%, and Rs. 2,000,000 is taxed under NTR. C) The entire Rs. 7,000,000 is taxable under the Normal Tax Regime (NTR) under the head 'Income from Other Sources' at applicable slab rates. D) Taxable under 'Income from Business' at 15%. Correct Answer: C Explanation: Any profit on debt exceeding Rs. 5 million derived by an individual is entirely taxable under the Normal Tax Regime (NTR) under the head 'Income from Other Sources' at the applicable progressive slab rates.
Question 11 If Zakat is deducted at the time of the receipt of profit on debt, how is this Zakat treated when computing the taxpayer's taxable income? A) It is claimed as a deductible allowance against the total taxable income of the person. B) It is allowed as a direct deduction against the gross 'Income from Other Sources' before applying the tax rate. C) It is treated as a tax credit to directly reduce the tax liability. D) Zakat deducted on profit on debt is entirely ignored for tax purposes. Correct Answer: B Explanation: In the case of profit on debt, Zakat paid under the Zakat & Ushr Ordinance, 1980 is allowed as a deduction in computing the income under the head "Income from Other Sources" itself, rather than as a general deductible allowance against total income.
Question 12 ABC Bank Limited’s core business is providing loans, and it earned Rs. 500 million as profit on debt during the year. Under which head of income will this amount be chargeable to tax? A) Income from Other Sources B) Final Tax Regime (FTR) C) Income from Business D) Capital Gains Correct Answer: C Explanation: Any profit on debt derived by a person where the person’s business is to derive such income (e.g., banks and financial institutions) shall be chargeable to tax under the head "Income from Business" and not under the head "Income from Other Sources".
Question 13 Mr. X rented a commercial building from the original owner. He later sub-leased a portion of this building to Mr. Y. Under which head of income will the rent received by Mr. X from Mr. Y be taxed? A) Income from Property B) Income from Business C) Income from Other Sources D) Exempt Income Correct Answer: C Explanation: Rent from the sub-lease of land or a building is specifically classified and chargeable to tax under the head "Income from Other Sources".
Question 14 In computing income under the head "Income from Other Sources", which of the following rules applies to the deduction of expenditures? A) Capital expenditures are fully allowed as deductions in the year they are incurred. B) Expenditures are allowed on an accrual (payable) basis regardless of actual payment. C) Expenditures paid for earning Income from Other Sources are allowed as deductions on a 'paid basis'. D) No deductions are allowed under this head under any circumstances. Correct Answer: C Explanation: In computing "Income from Other Sources", any expenditure paid for earning this income (other than capital expenditure) is allowed as a deduction. Because the basis of chargeability is 'receipt basis', the basis for the allowability of deductions is strictly 'paid basis'.
Question 15 A taxpayer leased out a building together with plant and machinery installed in it. Which of the following deductions can the taxpayer claim against this lease income under the head "Income from Other Sources"? A) Depreciation on the plant and machinery only. B) Initial allowance on the building. C) Depreciation on the plant, machinery, and building, and initial allowance on the plant and machinery only. D) Only actual repair and maintenance expenses. Correct Answer: C Explanation: In the case of income from the lease of a building together with machinery or plant, the taxpayer is allowed a deduction for depreciation on the plant, machinery, and building under Section 22, and an initial allowance on the plant and machinery only under Section 23.
